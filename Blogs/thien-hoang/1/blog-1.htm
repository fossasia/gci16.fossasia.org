Participating in Google Code-in 2016

Posted on November 30, 2016 by hoangvanthien

About a month ago, we had a very exciting meet-up with Ms. Dang Hong Phuc, who is one of the organizers of FOSSASIA — a group of developers from all around the world who desire to develop a very newly modern life based on open source. (Why don’t you pay a visit to their website?). She heated up the atmosphere of the meeting with a question “What do you want to do and to be in the future?”. Most of the students shared that Google was their ideal company. By the middle of the meeting, she had introduced herself, and FOSSASIA, and other open source communities. She focused on the important missions that was to bring knowledge freely to everyone, and allow people to contribute their grey-matter to each other.

share #opensource in #education #GoogleCode-In at Ly Tu Trong ‘former’ high school. So happy to be back. More events https://t.co/sf5ihwM5KQ pic.twitter.com/mUlA1qLDcb

We were informed how secondary school students at our ages were studying Technology in other countries. I was surprised at their ability to create such amazing robots and machines that would help human pass the threshold to a new era of Tech. Then she presented Google Code-in, a competition where under 18 year old students do tasks provided in order to use simple tools like GitHub, to get involved in Open Source project, and to do very challenging tasks eventually.

And that was the first time I know Google Code-in’s existence though its first contest was held 5 years ago. I am eager to discover open-source world more thoroughly, where my knowings of algorithm at school will be applied efficiently to human’s life.
