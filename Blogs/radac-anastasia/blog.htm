FOSSASIA and GCI

Posted on December 10, 2016 by anastasia2000

Hello again! As I wrote in my last blog post I go to The National College “Aurel Vlaicu” Orastie and  I`m in the math and informatics class. In my first year of high school, our IT teacher told us about Google Code In. At first I didn’t want to participate because I thought this tasks are really hard and I have to do only coding. That year I did just one task. Then, the next year, I realised how lucky I am because I can participate to this contest. For me, it was a nice experience. I got to know so many new things about so many different subjects. It really is a contest for every student who knows a bit of English.

Firstly, I improved my English claiming tasks and doing them every week  for a month and a half. Then, I found out about organizations like FOSSASIA, Drupal, OpenMRS, MusicBrainz, Apertium. Now, I am participating for the third time at the contest and I waited for him to begin since September. I am very excited to be in again. Also, I was tented to participate because of the prizes which are really interesting. Here I tell you more about them.

This contest is very interesting because of his prizes. Any participant which has completed at least one task, can win something. Then, if you complete 3 or more you win a T-shirt and a hoodie.  Another thing that I liked is that if you are selected as a winner, you can win a trip in the USA, California at Google`s headquarters.

As for FOSSASIA, to be honest, I found out about this organization reading this task. But it`s pretty cool to know more and more things about it and FOSS(Free and open-source software). So, about FOSSASIA I already know that is a non-porfit organization, founded by Hong Phuc Dang and Mario Behling in 2009. They are some of the member of FOSSASIA, at its beggining.

The aim of FOSSASIA is to develop  and adapt open technologies for social change with a focus on Asian users. The FOSSASIA Summit takes place annually one month after the Lunar New Year. Projects at FOSSASIA range from open hardware, to design, graphics and software. FOSSASIA offers resources to projects and organizes an annual Open Technology event for students, developers and start ups. Here is a picture taken in 2014 with the FOSSASIA group.

Here is a link of FOSSASIA: http://fossasia.org.

I hope you liked this blog post and now you to have a curiosity about Google Code In and FOSSASIA. Enjoy the contest!
